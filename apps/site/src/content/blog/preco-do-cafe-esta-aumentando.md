The article examines the global coffee crisis driven by climate change, particularly affecting Vietnam's robusta production and Brazil's arabica crops, resulting in significant price increases worldwide. It discusses how climate challenges—including drought conditions in Vietnam and reduced rainfall in Brazil's Minas Gerais region—are constraining coffee supplies, with Vietnam's coffee CEO reporting a 200% price increase in robusta coffee compared to previous years.
