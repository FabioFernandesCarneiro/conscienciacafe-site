The article explores the historical connection between Brazil's coffee industry and the arrival of football in the country. It traces how coffee exports necessitated railway construction by British engineers, whose presence also introduced football and other sports to Brazil. The piece explains how coffee export growth required infrastructure improvements, specifically the São Paulo Railway, bringing cultural influences that included football, which Charles Miller popularized after arriving in 1894.
