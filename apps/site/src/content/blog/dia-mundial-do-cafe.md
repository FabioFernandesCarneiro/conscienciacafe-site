The article celebrates World Coffee Day on April 14th, exploring how coffee has shaped cultures globally and examining coffee traditions in various countries, from Brazil to Ethiopia and Japan. It notes that after water, coffee is the most consumed beverage worldwide, with over 171.3 million bags consumed annually, covering unique coffee cultures including Brazil's status as the top producer, Indonesia's exotic Kopi Luwak, and Ethiopia's ceremonial coffee preparation called Buna Tetu.
